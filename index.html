Lajes Field, Azores, Portugal

My first assignment was quite a surprise. The Azores is an archipelago of nine islands located in the north Atlantic ocean off the coast of Portugal.

McGuire AFB Wrightstown, NJ

Next stop landed me on the East Coast in the small town of Wrightstown, NJ. Although I was assigned at McGuire AFB, I spent most of my time on the road. I was a Contingency Communications Radio Operator which meant where there was a war or possible contingency, my unit was there. Of course, back in the '90s they claim women didn't go to war.

Anderson AFB Guam

Guam was one of my favorite assignments. I gave birth to my first daughter and I had lots of fun on the beach. I also survived an 8.1 earthquake that was very devastating to the island infrastructure.

Scott AFB Belleville, IL

At Scott AFB I began my 8 year recruiting career. Recruiting is a very challenging but fulfilling position to have in the Air Force, at least if you're successful. I recruited young high schoolers for military service. After 3 years of recruiting for our enlisted forces, I was tasked to recruit college graduates to include pilots and engineers.

National Guard Bureau Crystal City, VA

This is where I started my career as an Air Force Officer. I was assigned as a Management Analyst at the Guard Bureau where I was very instrumental in the decision making process when determing the strength of each Air National Guard organization. This was a great learning experience which certainly came with some challenges.

The Pentagon Arlington, VA

At the Pentagon I worked as a Manpower Officer in Logistics. This was only a 1 year assignment but very enlightening to say the least. I had the opportunity to work with some very smart and influential government civilian employees. This was my first experience with working in the Logistics community.

Ali Al Salem Air Base Kuwait City, Kuwait

My deployment to Kuwait was my final adventure in the Air Force. In Kuwait I worked as a Protocal Officer. I met many dignitaries and celebrities during this tour. Although being a Protocal Officer is hard work, it's also very rewarding. It feels good to see the troops take some time out to relax and enjoy the entertainment. This was a great way to wrap up a 27 year Air Force career.
